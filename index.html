How these estimates work

This comparison illustrates how a household’s electricity costs can change over time when staying on standard utility service versus choosing a solar power option. The starting utility bill and solar payment are held constant, then a typical annual adjustment is applied to reflect changes in rates over time.

  • Utility costs are projected using a conservative 9% annual adjustment based on long-term price trends for the region.
  • Many solar agreements include a modest annual escalator. For reference, fixed escalators around 2.99% or 3.59% per year are common—choose the option that matches your proposal.
  • Use the timeline to review 1–30 years of expected cost exposure.

Figures shown are bill-to-bill estimates. Actual results may vary by usage, taxes, fees, and site conditions. Production and system upkeep are not reflected here. Please review your documentation for complete terms. © 2025 Residential Cost Comparison Tool.
